Getting Started

How do I join Kith?

Right now: Join the waitlist through our website. Scan the QR code or enter your email. You'll get a link to a quick survey (5 minutes) about your childcare needs, skills, and location.

Once approved for beta: We'll send you an invite link to download the app (iOS or Android). You'll complete onboarding (another 10 minutes) where you invite your first few trusted people and set up your profile.

Timeline: Private beta launches in 2026 for select cities. Public launch in 2027.

What domains does Kith support?

Currently available (Alpha): Childcare coordination—babysitting, school pickups, playdates, and emergency backup.

Coming in 2026-2027:

  • Learning (2026): Tutoring, skill-sharing, enrichment classes within your circle
  • Food (2027): Meal prep circles, bulk grocery runs, cooking together
  • Self-Care (2027): Yoga accountability, creative time, wellness circles
  • Eldercare (2027): Help with aging parents—appointments, companionship, errands

The key insight: 1 hour = 1 credit, regardless of domain. Help with childcare today, use that credit for eldercare next month. Your time has value across your entire life.

You can invite them! Kith works best when you bring your existing network onto the platform. During onboarding, you'll be able to send invite links to the 5-10 people you want to coordinate with most.

If they're not ready to join, you can still use Kith to track exchanges manually (you watched their kid for 4 hours → you note it in the app). When they join later, the history syncs.

Trust & Safety

How do I know I can trust people on Kith?

You build your own network. Kith isn't like other apps where you're matched with strangers. You invite people you already know and trust. They invite people they know and trust. Networks grow through vouching, not algorithms.

Trust signals:

  • See who vouched for someone (mutual connections)
  • View exchange history (do they follow through?)
  • Read private feedback from mutual friends
  • See reciprocity balance (are they giving and receiving fairly?)

You're always in control of who's in your circle. Remove anyone, anytime.

What if someone takes advantage of the system?

We protect against exploitation through:

  • Reciprocity tracking: If someone consistently takes without giving, the system flags it
  • Burnout warnings: If you're giving too much, we warn you before you hit empty
  • Private feedback: Report issues to admins without public drama
  • Removal tools: Anyone can remove anyone from their circle, no questions asked
  • Extended credit limits: You can't rack up infinite debt. System limits how much you can owe before requiring reciprocity

Remember: Kith is built on trusted networks. If someone's consistently taking advantage, they'll lose access as people remove them. Social accountability works.

What happens if there's a dispute?

Most disputes resolve through conversation. Kith provides tools to help:

  • Exchange logs: Both parties can see what was agreed vs. what happened
  • Mediation requests: Invite a mutual friend to help mediate
  • Credit adjustments: Both parties can propose adjustments if the exchange didn't go as planned

For serious issues: Contact our support team. We review both sides, check exchange history, and can facilitate resolution. In extreme cases, we can suspend accounts pending investigation.

For paid services: Standard protections apply. If you paid for childcare and it didn't happen, we refund you. If a provider claims non-payment, we check the logs. Clear accountability on both sides.

What about insurance and liability?

For unpaid exchanges (time banking): Kith is a coordination platform. We don't provide insurance for informal exchanges between friends. That's between you and your network—same as if you arranged it via text.

For paid services through our provider network:

  • Providers carry liability insurance (we verify this during vetting)
  • Background checks on all professional providers
  • Service guarantees: If service isn't provided as agreed, you get refunded
  • Incident reporting: Any issues are documented and reviewed

For direct member-to-member paid services: We facilitate payment but don't provide insurance. Members are responsible for their own coverage. We strongly recommend discussing expectations and safety protocols before any childcare arrangement.

Can I do background checks on network members?

For people you invite: These are your friends, family, neighbors—people you already know. We assume you're making trust decisions based on real relationships, not background checks.

For extended network (friends-of-friends): You can see who vouched for them and their exchange history. Trust is built through social proof and track record.

For professional providers in our vetted network: Yes. We run comprehensive background checks, verify licenses, check references, and require proof of insurance. These results are available to members before booking.

How It Works

What does "time banking" actually mean?

Simple: 1 hour of help = 1 hour of credit, regardless of what kind of help.

You watch someone's kids for 4 hours = you earn 4 time credits. Later, someone helps you move furniture for 2 hours = you spend 2 credits. Your balance: 2 credits remaining.

Key principle: All hours are valued equally. An hour of your time is worth an hour of anyone else's time. This prevents the "market rate" problem where some skills are valued over others.

Kith tracks this automatically. You don't do math or bookkeeping—just mark exchanges complete and we handle the rest.

What if someone can't return the favor?

Reciprocity doesn't have to be direct. You helped Sarah with childcare. Sarah doesn't owe you childcare back. She could:

  • Help you with job interview prep (she's in HR)
  • Teach your kid guitar (she plays in a band)
  • Give you rides to the airport (she works from home)
  • Help someone else in the network who can help you

Credit extends through your circle. If Sarah can't help you directly, she might help Marcus, who helps you. The network balances itself.

That said: if someone consistently can't or won't reciprocate, the system will flag it and suggest reducing exchanges with them.

How big should my circle be?

Sweet spot: 10-15 active people.

Research shows this is the number of people you can meaningfully coordinate with. Too few and you don't have coverage when you need it. Too many and coordination becomes overwhelming.

Start with 5-8 people you trust most. Grow gradually as needs arise. Quality over quantity—always.

What if I live somewhere rural without many families nearby?

Kith works differently in rural vs. urban settings, but it still works:

Rural advantage: People often already know each other and have informal helping networks. Kith just adds structure and tracking so you don't have to remember who owes what.

Consider: Your circle might include people 20 minutes away instead of 5. Skill exchanges (teaching, advice, digital help) work great even without proximity. And longer-term exchanges (watching kids for a weekend, not just 2 hours) might be more common.

We're designing the app to work for both dense urban networks and sparse rural ones.

Privacy & Data

What data do you collect?

We collect only what's needed to run the service:

  • Your profile info (name, location, skills, needs)
  • Your network connections (who you've invited, who invited you)
  • Exchange logs (what help was given/received, time credits)
  • Messages between members
  • Payment info (if you use paid services)

We don't collect: Browsing history, location tracking outside the app, third-party ad data, anything we don't need.

Do you sell my data?

Never. We make money from optional paid services, not surveillance capitalism.

Your network, your exchanges, your messages—none of it is for sale. Not to advertisers, not to data brokers, not to anyone. See how we make money →

Can I export my data?

Yes. At any time.

Export your full exchange history, time credits, messages, network connections, and profile data in standard formats (CSV, JSON). It's your data—you can take it with you if you leave Kith.

We also provide monthly reports formatted like a power bill: who you helped, who helped you, total hours, your balance. Makes it easy to see your activity at a glance.
