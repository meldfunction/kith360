Humans evolved to raise kids in groups of 8-14 adults

Most of us are doing it with 1-2 people. No wonder you're exhausted.

For 200,000 years, communities shared childcare, eldercare, food prep, and daily tasks. Then we isolated into separate houses and expected nuclear families to handle everything alone.

The burnout you feel isn't personal failure. It's a rational response to an impossible setup.

Two Ways to Get Help

Option 1

Your Circle

Coordinate with your 8-15 trusted people. Time credits keep it fair automatically. Covers 80% of your needs.

  • Fair exchanges (you help, they help)
  • Auto-tracked balance (no mental tallies)
  • Direct coordination (no strangers)
  • Works for any kind of help
  • Free forever

Starting with childcare (the most urgent need). Growing based on what YOUR circle coordinates.

Option 2

Provider Network

When your circle is tapped out. Background-checked professionals at fair rates. Covers the 20% your circle can't.

  • Background-checked caregivers

Sometimes you need help NOW and everyone's busy. That's when you hire from our vetted network.

You're always covered. Circle first, providers when needed.

Why Not Just Use...

Facebook Groups?

Problem:

No tracking. Who owes who? Guilt piles up. Requests get buried in group chat chaos.

Kith:

Auto-tracked balance. Direct 1:1 coordination. Everyone sees who's helped who. No guilt.

UrbanSitter?

Problem:

$25-35/hour adds up fast. Hiring strangers every time. 35% platform fee. No community building.

Kith:

Your people (free). Paid backup only when needed (5-15% fee vs 35%). Community builds over time.

Carefully?

Problem:

Fixed pods. Only childcare. What if your pod can't help? No paid backup option.

Kith:

Flexible circles. Any kind of help, not just childcare. Provider network backup. Grows with you.

Nextdoor?

Problem:

Public posts to strangers. No reciprocity tracking. Privacy concerns.

Kith:

Private circles. Fair tracking built-in. Trusted people only. You control who sees what.
