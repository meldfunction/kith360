Privacy Policy

Your data belongs to you. We're transparent about what we collect, how we use it, and how we protect it.

Our Privacy Principles

1. Your data is yours. You can export it anytime. You can delete it anytime. We don't own your information.

2. We don't sell your data. Ever. To anyone. Our business model is optional paid services, not advertising or data brokerage.

3. Zero-knowledge encryption. Your private messages are encrypted. We can't read them. Only you and your circle can.

4. Minimal collection. We only collect what we need to make the app work. No creepy tracking. No surveillance capitalism.

5. Transparency. This policy explains exactly what we do. If something changes, we'll tell you.

What We Collect

Account Information

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Phone number (optional, for SMS notifications)
  • Location (city/neighborhood for matching)
  • Profile photo (optional)

Circle Activity

To make time tracking work, we collect:

  • Exchange records (who helped who, when, for how long)
  • Time credit balances
  • Skills and availability you list
  • Requests for help you post
  • Reviews and ratings within your circle

Important: This information is only visible to your circle (the 8-15 people you've invited). Not to strangers. Not to the public. Just your circle.

Messages

Your direct messages with circle members are end-to-end encrypted. We can't read them. We store the encrypted data, but only you and the recipient have the keys to decrypt.

We do see metadata (who messaged who, when) to make the app function, but not message content.

Payment Information (If You Use Paid Services)

If you hire from our provider network:

  • Payment processing is handled by Stripe (PCI-compliant)
  • We never see your full credit card number
  • We only see transaction records (amount, date, provider)

Technical Data

Standard app usage data:

  • Device type (iOS/Android)
  • App version
  • Crash logs and error reports
  • Feature usage (which parts of the app you use)

We use this to fix bugs and improve the app. We don't use it for advertising.

What We Don't Collect

We explicitly do not collect:

  • Social security numbers
  • Financial account numbers (except through Stripe for payments)
  • Medical records
  • Browsing history outside the app
  • Contacts from your phone (unless you explicitly share to invite someone)
  • Location tracking when app is closed

How We Use Your Data

To Make the App Work

  • Match you with circle members who can help
  • Track time exchanges and credit balances
  • Send notifications about requests
  • Enable messaging between circle members
  • Process payments for provider network

To Improve Kith

  • Analyze which features people use (to prioritize development)
  • Fix bugs and crashes
  • Test new features with beta users

To Communicate With You

  • Send important updates (policy changes, security issues)
  • Respond to support requests
  • Send optional product updates (you can opt out)

How We Protect Your Data

Encryption

  • In transit: All data sent to/from our servers is encrypted (TLS/SSL)
  • At rest: Sensitive data stored on our servers is encrypted
  • Messages: End-to-end encrypted (zero-knowledge)

Access Controls

  • Only essential team members can access databases
  • All access is logged and monitored
  • Regular security audits

Data Minimization

  • We delete inactive accounts after 2 years
  • We purge old exchange records after 5 years (keeping only aggregated stats)
  • You can delete your account anytime (data deleted within 30 days)

Who We Share Data With

Your Circle

People you've invited to your circle can see:

  • Your profile (name, photo, skills, availability)
  • Your exchange history within the circle
  • Your credit balance
  • Requests you've posted

Service Providers

We use trusted third parties to run Kith:

  • Hosting: AWS (cloud infrastructure)
  • Payments: Stripe (payment processing)
  • Email: SendGrid (transactional emails)
  • SMS: Twilio (optional text notifications)
  • Analytics: PostHog (privacy-focused, self-hosted analytics)

These providers are contractually required to protect your data and can only use it to provide services to us.

Law Enforcement

We may disclose data if legally required (subpoena, court order). We'll notify you unless prohibited by law. We've never received a national security letter or FISA order.

We Never Share Data With:

  • Advertisers
  • Data brokers
  • Marketing companies
  • Social media platforms
  • Anyone else not listed above

Your Rights

Access Your Data

Request a copy of all data we have about you (we'll send within 30 days).

Export Your Data

Download your exchange history, messages, and profile data in standard formats (CSV, JSON).

Correct Your Data

Update your profile, skills, or any incorrect information anytime in the app.

Delete Your Data

Delete your account and all associated data. We'll purge everything within 30 days (except legally required records like payment receipts).

Opt Out of Communications

Unsubscribe from marketing emails (you'll still get critical security/policy updates).

Port Your Data

Take your data to another service (if one exists that's compatible).

Children's Privacy

Kith is not intended for children under 13. We don't knowingly collect data from children under 13. If we discover we have, we'll delete it immediately.

If you're a parent and believe your child has created an account, please contact us at [email protected].

International Users

Kith is based in the United States. If you use Kith from outside the US, your data will be transferred to and stored in the US.

We comply with applicable data protection laws, including GDPR for European users and CCPA for California residents.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as we add features or respond to legal requirements.

If we make significant changes: We'll email you 30 days before changes take effect. You can review and decide if you want to continue using Kith.

Minor updates: We'll note the change at the bottom of this page.
