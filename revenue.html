How We Make Money

1. Provider Network Fees (5-15%)

When you hire from our vetted provider network, we charge a platform fee. This covers:

  • Background checks (state & federal databases)
  • Reference verification
  • Insurance verification
  • Payment processing (Stripe fees)
  • Customer support
  • Ongoing provider monitoring

Why we charge you (not the provider): Other platforms take 20-35% from providers. We add 5-15% to what you pay. Providers keep their full rate. Everyone's happier.

2. Premium Features (Future)

Optional paid tier for power users who want:

  • Larger networks (coordinate with 20+ people)
  • Advanced analytics (track patterns, optimize balance)
  • Priority support (faster response times)
  • Custom integrations (calendar sync, etc.)

We'll announce pricing before launch. Circle coordination stays free regardless.

3. Enterprise Services (B2B)

Companies that want to offer Kith to employees:

  • Corporate childcare coordination
  • Employee resource groups
  • Community building tools

This doesn't affect individual users. Your circle stays private. Your data stays yours.

Why Not Charge Everyone?

We could charge $10/month for circle coordination. Lots of apps do. But we won't. Here's why:

1. Circles need critical mass. If only people who can afford $10/month join, you can't build your circle. Your neighbor might not have $10. Your coworker might not. Suddenly you're back to doing it alone.

2. Mutual aid shouldn't require money. The whole point is reducing financial burden by sharing help. Charging everyone to coordinate defeats the purpose.

3. We can build sustainably without it. Provider network fees + premium features + enterprise services = enough revenue to pay our team and grow the platform.

4. Free coordination aligns incentives. We succeed when your circle thrives. Not when we extract maximum subscription revenue. Better alignment = better product.

Most people use Kith for free. Some pay for providers or premium features. That subsidizes free circle coordination for everyone. It works.
